01 · The solution

One platform, three pieces

Application (60% ready)

The Abbott DemandFlow validated by the business — a ~40% development increment is needed to reach production — published as a premium application with role-based access and corporate sign-in (M365).

60% built+40% to go-live
Open intake, no license needed

Any employee submits via a requests app — built on SharePoint or a Power Apps Canvas app — feeding Intake Forms. The request enters the system on its own and the requester receives confirmation and status by email.

Automation & integration

DMC approvals via Teams and email, publication of the approved project in ServiceNow, and daily reading of the teams' real capacity.

02 · Automations

Seven flows that move demand on their own

1
Free intake
Requests flow in from the intake app with automatic impact scoring and requester confirmation.
2
Triage alert
New demand → immediate PMO notification via Teams and email.
3
DMC approval
Sequential: Sponsor → Tech Lead → Director. Decide from Teams or email, with a direct link.
4
Publish to ServiceNow
Approved demand → project created automatically with RCE, score and project number.
5
Status to requester
Every step forward → email with the new status. Transparency without a license.
6
Daily capacity
Automatic read of the teams' real capacity (FTE) from ServiceNow.
7
Alerts & backup
Overdue-demand alerts + weekly backup against accidental deletion.
03 · ServiceNow integration

What comes in, what goes out

IN ↓
Team capacity

FTE and committed hours for Internal, External and Support — every morning, to prioritize with real data.

OUT ↑
Approved project

Upon DMC approval: the project is created in ServiceNow with RCE, application code and score. The project no. returns to the Abbott DemandFlow.

SYNC ↔
Project status

Project progress in ServiceNow is reflected in the Abbott DemandFlow — a single source of truth for everyone.

Secure connection via the official ServiceNow API, with corporate credentials per environment (development → testing → production).
